## Users

Two interlocking profiles that often overlap:

- **Active DeFi trader**: operates on DEXs, manages wallets, hunts alpha in new token listings before the crowd. Wants speed and signal density. Judges tools by how fast they can act on information.
- **Mid-level crypto investor**: holds a diversified portfolio, wants clean performance tracking without technical overhead. Less interested in every price tick, more interested in risk-adjusted positioning.

Both users share one trait: they've outgrown Binance's clutter but find generic trackers too passive. They want a tool that respects their intelligence without overwhelming them.

## Product Purpose

Byrrgis is a mobile-first crypto trading and portfolio management app. Its core differentiator is the **Risk Score** — a proprietary per-token evaluation not available in other trackers or exchanges. Risk Score turns a noisy market into a legible decision surface.

Secondary differentiator: **Packs** — curated token baskets that let users gain thematic exposure without picking tokens one by one.

Success looks like: a user opening the app, scanning their portfolio health, evaluating a new token's Risk Score, and making a trade decision — in under 60 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Rápido · Nítido · Agresivo

Voice is direct and confident. No hedging, no softening. Speaks to traders as peers, not as customers to be reassured. Copy is terse and action-oriented. Numbers are the point; words support them.

## Anti-references

- **Binance and classic exchanges**: information overload, no visual hierarchy, everything competing for attention at once
- **Generic DeFi aesthetic**: neon green on pure black, hacker-terminal cliché, aggressive gradients as decoration
- **Conservative fintech**: navy + gold palette, bank-branch tone, passive voice, design that signals "safe" instead of "sharp"
- **Generic SaaS dashboard template**: big metric number, small label, gradient accent strip — immediately legible as AI-generated

## Design Principles

1. **Signal over noise.** Risk Score is the product's reason for existing — it must be immediately legible, never buried. Every other element is secondary.
2. **Speed is the feature.** Interactions feel instant. Transitions are purposeful and short. No decorative delays.
3. **Confident, not anxious.** Bold typography, decisive color. The UI doesn't hedge. It shows the data and trusts the user to act.
4. **Mobile-native, not mobile-ported.** Designed for thumb reach and tap/swipe — not a shrunk desktop layout. Every interaction maps to how people actually hold a phone.
5. **Scannable at pace.** A user in a volatile market needs to read portfolio performance in 3 seconds. Hierarchy is ruthless: the most important number is always the biggest and brightest.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG requirement at this stage. Prioritize contrast for legibility in varied lighting conditions (bright sunlight on mobile is a real use case). Avoid motion that could cause discomfort; keep animations short and easing-out only.
